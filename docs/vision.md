# Vision

Omoluabi is an open research initiative developing trustworthy infrastructure for observation, documentation, accessibility, provenance, consent, and civic knowledge. The project combines schema-first governance, accessible editorial intelligence, edge hardware, web infrastructure, and future APIs to support public-interest documentation across communities, journalists, researchers, educators, archives, and civic systems.

## Why Now

AI systems are increasingly able to generate, summarize, classify, and distribute information, but governance, consent, accessibility, provenance, and public accountability often remain afterthoughts. Omoluabi begins from the opposite position: public knowledge systems must be governed before they scale.

## Why Omoluabi

Public knowledge systems are under pressure from misinformation, inaccessible design, institutional opacity, extractive data practices, and ungoverned AI. Omoluabi begins with the premise that trustworthy systems must encode consent, provenance, accessibility, risk, correction, and human accountability from the start.

## Core Principle

> AI assists. Rules govern. Humans publish.

## Core Architecture Sentence

Rules produce schemas. Schemas produce interfaces. Interfaces produce software. Software produces public infrastructure.

Do not reverse this order.

## Non-Negotiables

1. Governance before AI.
2. Accessibility before polish.
3. Provenance before publication.
4. Human accountability before automation.
5. Rules before interfaces.
6. Schemas before apps.
7. Documentation before hype.
8. Public benefit before extraction.
9. Version history before erasure.
10. Transparency before recognition.
