## Core Question

> How do we protect stories from erasure?

Everything in Omoluabi begins here.

---

## The Story

Before archives,
there were stories around the fireside.

Communities remembered.

Elders listened.

Memory was our first library.

Some histories survive in institutions.

Others survive in people.

Through:

- oral history
- songs
- photographs
- family letters
- field notes
- maps
- witness testimony
- everyday conversation

These forms of memory are powerful.

They are also fragile.

---

## The Problem

Memory disappears.

Sometimes through conflict.

Sometimes through migration.

Sometimes through censorship.

Sometimes through neglect.

Sometimes through extraction.

Sometimes simply through time.

When systems fail,
stories are often the first thing erased.

---

## Omoluabi

Omoluabi is a civic memory system.

It listens carefully.

Captures responsibly.

Organizes transparently.

Reasons with evidence.

Documents provenance.

Supports accessibility.

Returns knowledge to communities.

Technology is not the goal.

Communities are.

---

## Field Companion

The Omoluabi Field Companion allows people to capture stories before they disappear.

Core capabilities include:

- Audio
- Video
- Images
- Notes
- Context
- GPS
- Time
- Consent
- Accessibility metadata
- Translation confidence
- Provenance
- Offline operation
- Long battery life
- Repairable hardware

---

## Omoluabi Platform

Captured stories become accountable memory.

Features include:

- Searchable transcripts
- Maps
- Source ladders
- Review workflows
- Accessibility checking
- Publishing
- Community archives
- Governance records

## Design Principles

We do not collect stories.

We protect them.

We do not replace communities.

We strengthen them.

We do not erase uncertainty.

We preserve it.

Accessibility is not an add-on.

It is infrastructure.

Human judgment remains central.

AI assists.

People decide.
