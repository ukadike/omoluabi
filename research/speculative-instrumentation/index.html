Omoluabi · Research

From Speculative Futures to Better Instruments

How UMADA scenarios expose failures in present-day observation systems and generate new requirements for Omoluabi.

Not lore. A research method.

UMADA is not marketing or worldbuilding surrounding Omoluabi. Within Omoluabi, it functions as a speculative research environment: future historical crises are built with enough material detail — dates, locations, described mechanisms of harm — that they can expose weaknesses in present-day observation instruments the same way a real case study would.

UMADA produces a traceable proof of method showing how speculative futures can expose weaknesses in present-day instruments and lead to actionable technical design requirements.

The process

Six steps, each one producing a document or artifact a reviewer can inspect directly — not a claim asked to be taken on faith.

  1. UMADA scenario
  2. Observational failure becomes visible
  3. Research question
  4. Technical requirement
  5. Omoluabi MVP change
  6. Documented design evidence

Text-form process diagram (no image); see the full method overview for the reasoning behind each step.

Case Study One: Photography

Future problem

A time traveler records an extraordinary event, but the image cannot prove what was seen.

Present-day question

What must a trustworthy field camera preserve beyond the image itself?

Omoluabi result

Original-file integrity, observer-versus-sensor distinction, orientation, capture conditions, alteration history, provenance, accessibility description, and confidence reporting — formalized as the Photographic Witness Layer.

Case Study Two: Cartography

Future problem

A place is destroyed, renamed, or erased, while winds and contamination move across oceans.

Present-day question

What must a trustworthy mapping instrument preserve beyond a visual map?

Omoluabi result

Canonical coordinates, datum, epoch, uncertainty, landscape memory, projection disclosure, movement traces, missingness, local naming, and protected-location governance — formalized as the Cartographic Witness Layer.
