Ontological Realities

This layer keeps different ways of knowing separate. It does not collapse evidence into belief or speculation into fact.

  • Evidence: what the official record contains.
  • Interpretation: what a reader or institution thinks the evidence may mean.
  • Hypothesis: a testable explanation that needs more evidence.
  • Belief: a meaning framework held by a community or individual.
  • Speculation: an imaginative possibility not yet supported by the record.
