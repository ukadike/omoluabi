WHAT IS IMPLEMENTED

One governed record, one universal media field

Media

Images, video, audio, and slideshows render through one reusable SSL component. Native semantic media remains available even when p5 adds an analysis layer.

Rules

Versioned SSL rules check provenance, epistemic separation, human review, alt text, transcripts, captions, autoplay, source status, and returnability.

Human authority

A passing rule check does not prove truth and does not publish a record. Human review and publication status remain separate decisions.

EPISTEMIC SEPARATION

Ontological realities

This layer keeps different ways of knowing visible rather than collapsing them into a single confidence or truth score.

Evidence
What a source record or media object contains.
Interpretation
What a reader, community, editor, or institution thinks the evidence may mean.
Hypothesis
A testable explanation requiring more evidence.
Belief
A meaning framework held by a community or individual.
Speculation
An imaginative possibility not yet supported by the record.
Fiction or synthetic media
Material intentionally created, reconstructed, simulated, or generated and labeled as such.
Unknown
Information that is missing, unresolved, inaccessible, or not yet reviewed.

Governing principle: Humans define the purpose. Rules constrain the system. Automation assists. Accountable people decide.
